About Me

I chose Computer Science because I love to create things. Ever since I was a little kid, I was always on the computer working on small little programs, games, and animations. This passion made my choice to go into Computer Science very easy.

This passion carried me through my time at UMD where I truly got to expand my knowledge. I studied new programming languages, algorithms, and technologies all with the same love I had for it as when I was a kid. It was also in this time where I got my first experience in the industry with my internship at Dell. The experience I had there was incredible, and I learned more than I thought I ever would. Working with a team of experienced developers really showed me what working in that kind of environment was like. I dug deep into things such as C#, SQL, and Node.js. I also learned about the Agile development process with things like Kanban. I learned so much from my summer there, and I couldn't wait to learn more.

My other work experience has given me another set of useful skills. Lifeguarding and being a barista are very customer facing environments, meaning you are always interacting with people and on your feet. My work at a cabinet shop really showed me what hard work was like, and how rewarding it can be when you try your best.

Now, working as a Software Engineer, the passion to learn and create remains with me. I have learned so much up to this point, but there is still so much to explore.
